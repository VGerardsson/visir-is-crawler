ABOUT US

ICELAND FRÉTTIR is an e-learning ressource for everyone learning icelandic. Frontpage news is freshly collected every day from different icelandic newspapers. The difficulty level of every piece is depicted right next to it. This will help students find suitable articles at their own level.

The goal of this website it to help foreign students learning Icelandic find every day articles. It is also a useful learning ressource for teachers that need to select interesting articles for their students.

DIFFICULTY LEVELS

The news is indexed on a daily basis to allow students to stay up-to-date with what is happening in Iceland within the limits of their individual reading comprehension. The difficulty level is determined by the Automated Readability (AR)-Index. For more information on this indexation method please check out Wikipedia here.

The estimated AR-Index values shown in the following table are compared to different age and language levels. The language levels A1 to C2 correspond to CEFR-levels (Common European Framework of Reference for Languages).

The numbers for the estimated active vocabulary and the age of native speakers are taken from different sources. The vocabulary used in real life by different age groups varies tremendously.
